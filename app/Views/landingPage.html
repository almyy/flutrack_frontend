Right now, there are no cities with an epidemic, but here's an example of how the spread would have looked if there were an epidemic in Honolulu!

This web page is part of the results from a Master's thesis written by Martin Almvik and Mikael Rino Solstad at NTNU. The system scans tweets, and calculates and visualizes the spread of a potential influenza pandemic from a certain city, given that a city has experienced an influenza epidemic. The slider above chooses which day to visualize on the map.

The table below contains all the city represented in the system, and shows how many influenza related tweets have been found from each of them. If a city has an increasing trend, it's highlighted in yellow, while it's highlighted in red if our system has detected an influenza epidemic in that city.
